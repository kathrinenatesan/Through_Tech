Online Safety: Secure Passwords

October 19, 2024 3 Min Read

Many digital apps and websites often ask users to come up with a password and in order to keep your personal information safe when using the internet, it is important to create strong and secure passwords for your accounts.

What Makes a Strong Password?

Try to come up with a password that is at least 12 characters long; combines uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols; and consider even using a password generator to help you come up with strong passwords (https://privacycanada.net/strong-password-generator/).

Make sure to stay away from these things when coming up with passwords:

Do not include personal information such as birthdays, names of pets or children, your phone number, address information, etc. (Example: Edward2008 because it contains a name and a date which is personal information that could be easy to guess).

Do not use overused sequences such as “password” or “1234”

Try to come up with different passwords for different accounts that might be important to keep all your accounts secure.

Avoid using any real words that can be found in a dictionary (Ex: hiking6 because words in a dictionary should be avoided since these passwords can quickly be revealed with the right software).

How Can I Keep My Password Secure?

Never share passwords with anyone and be cautious with sensitive information, avoid sending passwords via email or digital messaging, create unique passwords for each account, and enable multi-factor authentication for your accounts to make sure you know who is signing in and using your account.
