What are the most common scams?

Common scams include:

Robocalls, lottery scams, job offer scams, online dating scams, messaging scams, fake website links, and more.

Fortunately, with good internet safety practices, you can protect your privacy and avoid falling for these scams.

1. Be Wise With Personal Information

Always be cautious of sharing personal information such as bank account information, social security, addresses, etc. online, especially to someone you donâ€™t know.

2. Use Strong Passwords

Strong passwords are important for keeping your accounts and personal information secure and harder to access through other means. To read more about creating strong passwords, feel free to refer to the article "Online Safety: Secure Passwords."

3. Avoid 3rd Party Streaming Services

Although using these convenient websites might seem better than paying your monthly Netflix subscription, many of these websites are not secure and may be trying to access your personal information.

4. Avoid Clicking Unknown Links

Fraudulent links are a common type of phishing scam and users should be careful when coming across unreliable emails or links in general.

5. Think Twice Before Believing Anything On the Internet

In general, this is a good rule to have for anything you might come across on the internet, but especially when it comes to scams, scammers will often lure users with tempting job offers, lottery prizes, and discounts. Be careful and stay cautious about these offers if it involves your personal information.
